About Me

I am Stefano, a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Istituto Italiano di Tecnologia and a visiting researcher at the University of Cambridge in the group led by Professor Pietro Lioâ€™. My journey in academia is fueled by my passion for pushing the boundaries of knowledge and using advanced computational methods to solve real-world problems.

I obtained my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Milano-Bicocca in 2023, where I developed a strong foundation in machine learning, graph theory, and interdisciplinary applications spanning from computational chemistry to smart mobility. Along the way, I honed my skills during internships and research visits, including an impactful stint at Nokia Bell Labs in Cambridge (2021) and a research visit to the Mathematics Department at the University of Southampton (2022).

My research interests are as diverse as they are interconnected. I am deeply involved in the exploration of Graph Neural Networks and Hypergraph Neural Networks, which help in understanding complex data structures, as well as advancing methods in Computer Vision, Computational Chemistry, Diffusion Models, and Smart Mobility. This multidisciplinary focus enables me to approach problems from new angles and collaborate with experts across various domains.

Beyond my research, I thrive on continuous learning and am always enthusiastic about collaborating with diverse research groups. I believe that fruitful collaborations and the exchange of ideas are key to catalyzing innovation and scientific progress.

When I am not immersed in research, you can often find me outdoors. I have a deep passion for hiking and skiing, activities that not only refresh my mind but also inspire creative thinking. I enjoy traveling to different countries, experiencing new cultures, and reading about a wide range of topics, all of which contribute to my broad perspective and lifelong commitment to learning.
